History of the World

World War I and II

France
The impact of World Wars on France

France played a major role in both World War I and II. From the battles of the Marne to the Liberation of Paris, the country's involvement shaped the course of these conflicts.

During World War I, France was one of the principal Allies. The Western Front was a primary theater of war, and many significant battles took place on French soil. In World War II, France faced occupation by Nazi Germany until the D-Day landings and the subsequent liberation by Allied forces.

Germany
Germany's role in World Wars

Germany's role in the world wars was central and significant. As the leading Central Power in World War I and the principal Axis Power in World War II, Germany's actions had profound effects on the global stage.

In World War I, Germany's military strategies and the extensive use of trench warfare defined much of the combat on the Western Front. In World War II, under the leadership of Adolf Hitler, Germany initiated the war with the invasion of Poland and later orchestrated the Holocaust, which resulted in the genocide of six million Jews.

Czech Republic
Czech lands in the World Wars

The Czech lands, part of Austria-Hungary during World War I and Czechoslovakia during World War II, experienced significant turmoil and transformation due to these conflicts.

During World War I, many Czechs fought for the Austro-Hungarian Empire, while others joined the Czechoslovak Legions fighting for the Allies. In World War II, the German occupation of Czechoslovakia led to severe repression, resistance movements, and ultimately the country's liberation by Allied forces.

Portugal
Portugal's involvement in the World Wars

Portugal's involvement in the World Wars was unique. As a relatively small nation, Portugal's contributions were nonetheless significant in both conflicts.

In World War I, Portugal joined the Allies and sent troops to the Western Front, where they fought in battles such as La Lys. In World War II, Portugal remained neutral but provided strategic support to the Allies, including leasing air bases in the Azores, which were crucial for the Atlantic war effort.
